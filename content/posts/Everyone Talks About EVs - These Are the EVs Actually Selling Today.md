## The real EVs that are making a killing in the market, very silently.

Last evening, I ordered pizza through Zomato. The delivery partner reached my door on time.

Now, since I have an entry control app and the guard at the gate also calls in to confirm, I have enough bells and whistles to know when a delivery person arrives. I also check from my window facing the road to see the moment he reaches my door, and I open the door to receive the items.

---

## The Silent Realization

But this time, after receiving the parcel and closing the door, I had a nagging feeling that something was wrong.

I am a very cautious person (as you might've noticed), and so my brain is tuned to noticing something that was missing once I closed the door - I did not hear the person leave my premises. I did not hear him drive off.

Upon inspection, I noticed that the poor fellow had indeed driven away, and the reason I did not hear him was that he had come in on an EV scooter.

I had noticed it earlier, but it clearly did not register, and my subconscious was eagerly waiting to hear the drumming of an old motorbike engine being started, or the scratchy noise of a starter - both of which do not exist on an EV, by the way.

---

## A Familiar Yet Ignored Shift

Feeling stupid over being overly cautious and for doubting an innocent fellow, I pondered further over dinner.

This wasn't the first time I encountered an electric scooter - in fact, almost all my uncles back in my village were riding one nowadays. I had even discussed how silent his electric scooter was with my cousin during our last visit to our farmhouse.

He had borrowed it from his friends there, and it was his first time on an EV too.

---

## What Did I Miss?

So, if I was so aware of EV scooters and bikes, and have noticed many delivery persons in Bangalore alone driving an EV scooter, what tripped me last night?

Turns out, apart from my anxiety disorder, the fact that I've recently only been focusing on EV cars got my mind to completely ignore EV scooters.

---

## The Market Blind Spot

Trust me, that's exactly what is happening in the market too.

There is a lot of noise around EV cars that almost everyone misses the fact that there is a 2-wheeler category of EVs that is actually making a killing, silently.

---

## Why This Article

That's when it struck me - why not talk about the EV 2-wheeler market, and hence this blog.

EV scooters and bikes deserve the same respect and space as any EV 4-wheeler would on this site.

So, let's go - let's discuss 2-wheelers for a while. Figure out what's different and whom it is for.

---

## What EV Two-Wheelers Actually Change

"It's not just a scooter, but it's electric" - that's the baseline.

Compared to traditional petrol scooters and bikes, these are very different in the way they start, ride, and recharge (instead of refuel).

And not to mention the number of integrations and low-key tech that's readily made available on these platforms. Being electric makes you look at this segment very differently.

---

## Maintenance Advantage

I hope that by now (based on my previous articles), you would've already realised that these 2-wheelers do not have any engine or gearbox, so there is no oil change or filter change that's needed, unlike their petrol-fuelled counterparts.

That means:

- Fewer maintenance headaches  
- No frequent fuel stops  

---

## Battery & Range: The Only Specs That Matter

Like electric cars, electric 2-wheelers are also driven by an electric motor placed in the wheel hub or mounted to the chassis - similar to how an engine is mated in a traditional scooter.

The electric motor is paired with a battery pack - smaller than cars - that powers the vehicle.

---

### Motor Power

- Typical scooters: **2–4 kW BLDC motor**  
- Premium scooters: **5–8 kW motors**  

Cars use motors with **10–15× higher capacity**.

---

### Battery Placement

- Under floorboard (skateboard-style)  
- Under seat storage  
- Removable battery under seat (budget EVs)  

---

### Battery Capacity & Range

- Battery sizes: **1 kWh → 5–6 kWh**  
- Sweet spot: **2–4 kWh**  

Approximate range:

- **30–40 km per kWh**  

Examples:

- 2.5 kWh → ~75–100 km  
- 5.3 kWh → ~150–200 km (claimed, real-world lower)  

---

## Charging: Where Most Decisions Are Made

Here's the best part about owning an electric 2-wheeler - charging straight from home.

- Plug into any wall socket  
- Optional fast charging (Ather Grid, etc.)  

---

### Running Cost Comparison

- EV: ~₹0.25–₹0.30/km  
- Petrol scooter: ~₹2–₹2.5/km  

**~8–10× cheaper to run**

## The Troublesome Part

While I did mention low maintenance costs, there are challenges.

Because it's a relatively new platform:

- Quality issues may exist  
- Service challenges are real  

---

### Key Concerns

- Limited trained EV mechanics  
- Spare parts availability  
- Longer repair wait times  

You cannot always rely on local garages yet.

---

### Improving Scenario

If you choose:

- Legacy brands (TVS, Bajaj)  
- Strong EV-first brands (Ather)  

Service and support are improving steadily.

If you cut through all the noise and focus on what really matters:

EV 2-wheelers make a lot of sense.
