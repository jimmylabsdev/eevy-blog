## The Best Car My Friend Ever Bought

A close friend of mine bought a compact hatchback a few years ago.

Nothing flashy.

No panoramic sunroof.

No giant touchscreen.

No fancy connected features.

Just a simple, reliable car.

Everyone had an opinion.

"You should've bought the bigger one."

"Why didn't you stretch your budget a little?"

"For a few lakhs more, you could've got an SUV."

He smiled every time and said the same thing.

"It does exactly what I need."

A few years later, I realised he had made the smartest decision among all of us.

Because he hadn't bought the **best** car.

He had bought the **right** car.

I think the same applies to EVs today.

## Every Lifestyle Is Different

Think about it.

Some people drive less than 20 kilometres a day.

Others spend three hours every day commuting through city traffic.

Some have dedicated parking at home.

Some rely entirely on apartment parking.

Some travel across states every month.

Others rarely leave the city.

Some are buying their first family car.

Others are replacing a perfectly good petrol vehicle simply because they want lower running costs.

All of them are shopping for an EV.

Yet none of them should necessarily buy the same one.

---

## Start With Your Daily Life

Before looking at brochures or specifications, spend a few minutes thinking about your own routine.

Ask yourself simple questions.

How far do I usually drive every day?

How often do I travel outside the city?

How many people usually travel with me?

Where will I charge the car?

How much am I comfortable spending every month?

You'll probably notice that the answers come much more easily than comparing battery sizes and motor outputs.

And surprisingly, they tell you far more about the kind of EV you actually need.

---

## Bigger Isn't Always Better

It's natural to assume that a larger battery is the better choice.

More range sounds reassuring.

But larger batteries also increase the cost of the vehicle.

If most of your driving happens within the city, you may never fully utilise that additional capacity.

On the other hand, if your weekends regularly involve highway drives, you'll probably appreciate every extra kilometre.

Neither choice is right or wrong.

They're simply designed for different lifestyles.

---

## Think About Ownership, Not Just The Purchase

Choosing an EV isn't only about the day you bring it home.

It's about the years that follow.

Charging.

Running costs.

Insurance.

Maintenance.

Road trips.

Family holidays.

Daily office commutes.

School runs.

When you look at the complete ownership journey instead of only the purchase price, your decision becomes much clearer.

---

## The Right EV Is The One That Fits Your Life

I think that's where many of us get stuck.

We spend weeks comparing cars.

Very little time comparing our own needs.

Once you understand how you actually use a vehicle, the shortlist almost creates itself.

Instead of trying to choose between ten different EVs, you may suddenly find that only two or three genuinely fit your lifestyle.

And that's a much easier decision to make.
