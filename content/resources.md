## Tools and references to make better EV decisions

This page contains practical tools and structured guides to help you evaluate EVs in India using real numbers and real usage patterns.

## 1. EV Cost Reality (Simple Model)

Before buying an EV, this is the only calculation that matters:

**Are you actually saving money?**

Use this quick framework:

### Step 1 — Monthly running

- Daily usage (km): ___
- Monthly km = daily × 30

---

### Step 2 — Cost per km

**Petrol:**
- Mileage: ~40 km/l (2-wheeler typical)
~13 km/l (4-wheeler typical)
- Petrol price: ₹100/l  
→ Cost per km ≈ ₹2.5

**EV:**
- Efficiency: ~35–45 km per kWh  
- Electricity: ₹8/kWh  
→ Cost per km ≈ ₹0.20

---

### Step 3 — Monthly cost

- Petrol cost = km × ₹2.5  
- EV cost = km × ₹0.20  

---

### Step 4 — Savings

- Monthly savings = Petrol − EV  
- Yearly savings = × 12  

---

### Step 5 — Break-even

- Extra cost of EV ÷ yearly savings = years to recover

---

## Reality check

- Low usage (<20 km/day) → savings are slow  
- High usage (>40 km/day) → EV makes strong sense  
- Charging access matters more than range  

---

## 2. Charging Reality in India

### Home charging (ideal case)

- Works best for: apartments with parking + socket  
- Overnight charging = sufficient for most users  

---

### Public charging (current state)

- Still inconsistent, but rapidly expanding  
- Useful as backup, not primary strategy  

---

### What actually matters

- Do you have reliable home charging?  
→ If not, reconsider EV  

---

## 3. Battery & Range — Simplified

- Bigger battery (kWh) = more range  
- Real range is ~70–80% of claimed  
- Speed, load, and riding style matter  

---

## 4. When NOT to buy an EV

- Very low usage  
- No charging access  
- Buying only for “fuel savings” without calculation
