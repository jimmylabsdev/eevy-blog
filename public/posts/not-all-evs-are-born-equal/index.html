Born Electric vs ICE-Derived Platforms

I spent a major part of my childhood in Delhi, before relocating down south to Trichy, our hometown, due to dad’s transfers, of course. Delhi and Trichy used to have a common mode of transport during the 80’s - the rickshaw. No, I’m not not talking about the ‘Tuk-Tuk’, but the cycle rickshaw.

It was more or less a tricycle with some seating arrangements for the passengers. I’m sure this was common across India and maybe still exists in some corners. But it needed a lot of manual effort to be able to operate these. It was like riding a cycle, but with 2-3 passengers riding at the back, it must have been a tough ride.

From Muscle to Motor

Some genius thought about this and came up with a solution to this problem by attaching a petrol motor to this contraption. Thus making it a motorized cycle rickshaw which then eventually phased out to the much more sophisticated and better designed - autorickshaw or ‘Tuk-Tuk’.

Another instance of such conversion is when most of us in India started running our 800s and Omni using LPG instead of petrol. All it needed was a simple kit to be installed.

Conversions Are Not New

Point is - such conversions for simple convenience is not new to us. We have done it in the past, we are doing it today and will eventually be doing it in the future too.

Wait. We’re doing it now? - Yes and No, and here’s how.

Two Types of EVs

Today, we have two types of EVs:

  • Ones that are born electric
  • Ones that are converted to electric

More like a muggle vs pure-bred relationship.

Born Electric

These are EVs based on platforms built from the ground-up specifically designed for being an Electric Vehicle.

What does that mean?

These are platforms that are built keeping EV specific needs in mind like:

  • Space for battery and its placement
  • Weight distribution

These platforms are built around a skateboard architecture, where the battery pack becomes a part of the chassis as it is mated to the floor.

The battery layout, the wheelbase and others are designed to be EV-optimized right from the get-go.

ICE-Derived EVs

These are platforms built with a heavy engine and a fuel tank in mind.

Battery packs are stacked where possible:

  • Engine compartment
  • Trunk
  • Under the seat
  • Wherever there’s space to spare

These batteries alone can weigh around 400 - 600 kgs all put together, so weight distribution becomes very tricky.

And then there is another level of engineering or re-engineering that goes into adapting the ICE based architecture to EVs:

  • Software integration
  • Thermal management
  • Structural reinforcement

Are Derived EVs Inferior?

While it’s not like derived EVs are inferior to a born electric, but born electric platforms do give more importance to:

  • Battery placement
  • Thermal protection
  • Environmental safety
  • Impact protection

Real-World Examples

Let’s look at a few born electric and derived EVs without making this anymore technical.

BE6 - What a Born Electric Looks Like

Mahindra & Mahindra’s BE6 could probably be one of the first modern, mass-market EVs that was built from scratch as an EV - only if we conveniently ignore the ‘Reva’.

Reva is what probably started all this, atleast in India.

But without dwelling too much into the past and make this too boring, lets move on.

The BE 6 is Mahindra’s clean break from the past.

  • No engine legacy
  • No compromise packaging
  • Just an EV designed as an EV

What You’ll Notice

  • The wheels are pushed outwards → more cabin space
  • The floor is flat → better rear seat comfort
  • There’s no transmission tunnel bump
  • Proportions look different from typical SUVs

These are small things individually, but together they tell you:

This car wasn’t adjusted to become electric - it started electric.

Tata Nexon EV - The Derived Approach

Now let’s compare that with something more familiar - Tata Nexon EV.

It’s a good car. Practical. Proven. Everybody loves it - almost everybody.

But it wasn’t originally designed as an EV.

What Changes?

  • The basic shape stays the same as the petrol version
  • Space is worked around, not optimized
  • Slight tunnel or packaging compromises may exist
  • No extra storage like a frunk

Again - nothing “wrong” here.

Just a different starting point.

Market Reality

In India today:

  • Lower-cost EVs → typically ICE-derived
  • Higher-cost EVs → closer to born-electric platforms

Why This Matters

Both approaches work.

  • One is engineered forward
  • The other is adapted smartly

But when you understand where a car comes from, you start noticing:

  • Space
  • Balance
  • Driving feel

That’s where the real difference starts to show.

Born Electric vs ICE-Derived - What Actually Changes

1. Space & Packaging

  • Born Electric
    Designed around the battery → flat floor, better legroom, more usable cabin space

  • ICE-Derived
    Space is adjusted around existing structure → small compromises in legroom and layout

2. Weight Distribution

  • Born Electric
    Battery sits low and centered → better balance, more stability

  • ICE-Derived
    Batteries placed wherever space is available → balance needs extra engineering effort

3. Driving Feel

  • Born Electric
    Feels planted and predictable → weight is evenly spread

  • ICE-Derived
    Can feel slightly uneven depending on how weight is distributed

4. Design Freedom

  • Born Electric
    Designers get a clean slate → proportions look different, more futuristic

  • ICE-Derived
    Design stays close to petrol/diesel version → familiar, but constrained

5. Engineering Complexity

  • Born Electric
    Built for EV from day one → fewer compromises

  • ICE-Derived
    Requires re-engineering → structure, cooling, safety all need adjustments

6. Cost & Accessibility

  • Born Electric
    Usually more expensive → newer platforms, higher investment

  • ICE-Derived
    More affordable → existing platforms reused smartly

So, Which One Should You Choose?

If you’re looking for practicality and value, ICE-derived EVs make a lot of sense.

If you want the full EV experience - better space, balance, and future-ready design - born electric platforms are where things are headed.
